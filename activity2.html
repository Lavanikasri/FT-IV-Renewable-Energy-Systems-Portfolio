Explanation

The hybrid renewable energy system integrates solar and wind technologies to supply continuous and clean power to a rural health clinic. The solar PV array converts sunlight to DC energy, while the wind turbine generates power from moving air. Both sources are managed by a hybrid charge controller that optimizes charging of the battery bank. The stored energy is converted into AC through an inverter and used for medical loads, lights, and refrigeration. A backup generator ensures reliability during extended low-resource conditions. This hybrid system enhances energy resilience, reduces diesel dependency, and promotes sustainable healthcare in off-grid areas.
