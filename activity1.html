Explanation

A rooftop solar photovoltaic (PV) system uses solar energy as its renewable source. The PV panels convert sunlight into direct current (DC) electricity through the photovoltaic effect. The generated DC is regulated by a charge controller that safely charges the battery bank, which stores energy for nighttime use. The stored power or direct solar output is converted to alternating current (AC) using an inverter, making it suitable for domestic loads. The system also includes a grid connection that allows two-way power flow—exporting excess power to the grid and importing power when solar energy is insufficient. This 5 kW setup supports a household’s energy demand, reduces electricity costs, and promotes sustainable energy independence.
